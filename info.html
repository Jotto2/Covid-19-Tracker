Symptoms

You can be sick for 1 to 14 days before symptoms develop. The most common symptoms of the coronavirus (covid-19) are fever, fatigue and dry cough. Most (about 80%) get healthy without needing treatment.

The disease can rarely be severe and even fatal. Elderly people with other illnesses (such as asthma, diabetes or heart disease) are more likely to become seriously ill.

Symptoms can be

  • cough
  • fever
  • fatigue
  • difficulty breathing (severe cases)

Prevention

There is currently no vaccine for the coronavirus (COVID-19).

You can protect yourself and help prevent the spread of the virus by doing the following:

Remember to

  • wash your hands regularly for 20 seconds with soap and water or alcohol-based hand spray
  • cover your nose and mouth with a paper towel or use your elbow when coughing or sneezing
  • avoid close contact (1 meter) with people who are ill
  • Stay home and isolate yourself from others in the home if you feel ill

Avoid to

  • Touch your eyes, nose or mouth if your hands are not clean

Treatment

There is no medicine to prevent or treat the coronavirus (COVID-19). Some patients will need treatment in hospitals, including breathing help.

If you have mild symptoms, you should stay home until you are healthy. You can relieve the symptoms by resting and sleeping, staying warm, drinking plenty of fluid, using a humidifier or taking a hot shower to relieve sore throats and coughs.

If you have a fever, cough, or difficulty breathing, seek medical help immediately. Call before going to the doctor's office and tell if you have recently been traveling or been in contact with travelers.
